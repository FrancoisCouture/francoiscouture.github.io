Calculator

Here's one of my schematics. It is a calculator shield that is intended to placed on top of an Arduino, as indicated by the headers.

The i2C pullups connects the unused i2C pins to the dc supply, keeping the input high.

The EEPROM is an erasable chip that contains the calculator program.

The relay detects a small incoming signal to allow a larger dc flow. When power is fed through the relay, the EEPROM turns on.

There are six screens connected to a TM1637 for timing, brightness control and display refresh. Bigger numbers than six digits are impossible: The display LEDS are too big.

Finally, there are 16 programmable buttons available, 10 of which are reserved for numbers.
